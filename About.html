◆ Manifesto · Filosofi

Made by hand, for slow people in a fast world.

I started Cute Things by Eca in my parents' garage in 2019 with one cutting board, one awl, and a half-hide of vegetable-tanned cowhide. My first tote took me two weeks and ninety-four stitches more than it should have. I sold it to a friend for less than it cost me to make.

Six years later, we are four people working out of a small studio in Bandung. We still make every piece by hand. We still only use full-grain leather from Indonesian tanneries. We still refuse to rush.

We are not trying to be the biggest. We want to be the atelier your daughter inherits your bag from.Kami bukan toko besar. Kami atelier kecil yang ingin tas kami diwariskan.

◆ What we believe · Yang kami percaya

Four rules we won't break.

I

Full-grain onlyKulit utuh saja

We never use split, bonded, or corrected leather. Only the top layer of the hide, with all its character intact.

II

Saddle-stitchedJahit tangan

Two needles, waxed thread, by hand. Slower than a machine, but a single cut thread won't unravel the whole seam.

III

Solid brassKuningan padat

No plated hardware that peels in a year. Solid brass that develops a warm patina instead.

IV

Forever repairReparasi seumur hidup

Bring it back frayed, scratched, or torn. We'll fix it. Free, for as long as we exist.

Six years, one bench.

A TikTok finds usTikTok menemukan kami
A 28-second clip of edge-burnishing goes viral. Our first 40 orders come in over a weekend. We run out of leather.
2021
Two more handsDua tangan lagi
Rani joins to help with stitching. We move into a proper studio in Dago. The garage becomes a photo spot.
2023
Garut tannery partnershipKemitraan di Garut
We start working directly with a family-owned tannery in Garut that also supplies a famous European house. Same hide, one pair of hands away.
2024
14 pieces, made to order14 model saja
We narrow our catalog to 14 core pieces. Every order is cut the week it's placed. No warehouses, no leftovers.
2026
Still small, on purposeTetap kecil, dengan sengaja
Four makers. Twelve leathers. One bench. Over 2,400 pieces sent to homes across Indonesia and 18 other countries.
